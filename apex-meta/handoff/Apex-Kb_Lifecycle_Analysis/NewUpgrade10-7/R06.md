research_report:  
id: R06

verdict: >  
A connector-readable authoring checklist can prevent every currently encoded  
quality reason, but it cannot independently prove exact equivalence to  
`quality --strict`. Exact equivalence requires executing the Python command  
because tokenization, section extraction, YAML parsing, regex counting,  
manifest comparison, and exit behavior are executable semantics rather than  
ordinary document-reading semantics.

checks_not_reliably_connector_observable:  
- >  
Exact `_quality_words` totals after fenced-code and inline-code removal,  
including the Python regular expression's Unicode token boundaries.  
- >  
Exact `_quality_section` boundaries, especially with repeated headings,  
nonstandard heading levels, malformed Markdown, or headings inside fences.  
- >  
Exact frontmatter interpretation under PyYAML versus the fallback minimal  
parser.  
- >  
Exact claim, ranked-source, route, and pointer counts produced by the  
implementation regular expressions.  
- >  
Exact pointer classification when a value contains ambiguous numbers,  
fragments, section terms, or path syntax.  
- >  
Whether `source_refs` match manifest identifiers, and whether references  
are reported as unmanifested, without reading and applying the manifest  
normalization logic.  
- >  
Process exit status and strict-mode behavior. Connector inspection cannot  
substitute for running `quality --strict`.  
- >  
Whole-KB coverage outputs such as manifest sources without pages and  
source-to-page maps.

checklist_text_under_250_words: |  
Before committing a Phase 2 summary, concept, or entity page:

```
1. Use valid frontmatter and include at least one usable `source_refs` entry.
2. Include these exact headings: Adaptive Ranked Source Set; Macro / Meso /
   Micro; Key Claims; Routes Here; Uncertainty / Raw Source Reopen Triggers.
3. Remove all `<placeholder>` text, TODO, TBD, and “LLM must fill.”
4. Under Key Claims, use structured `claim_id:` or `claim:` entries. Give
   every counted claim a `source_pointer:` or `pointer:`.
5. Summaries need at least two counted claims and two ranked-source entries.
6. Concepts with Macro, Meso, and Micro each under 20 parsed words need at
   least two claims.
7. A concept whose Micro subsection is under 20 parsed words needs at least
   one section-level or line/span-level claim pointer.
8. For summaries and concepts, Macro, Meso, and Micro must not all be under
   20 parsed words. Narrow entities are exempt from this depth rule.
9. Routes Here needs at least one `question:` or `leads_to:` entry.
10. Run `python apex-meta/scripts/apex_kb.py --kb-root <root> quality
    --strict --json`. The checklist prevents known reasons; only the command
    is authoritative.
```
