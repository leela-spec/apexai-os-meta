## Core Insight
I realized energetically that my anger, frustration, and irritation often cover grief.

## Mechanism
- **Surface state:** anger, frustration, tension
- **Hidden state:** grief, sadness, self-compassion
- **Protective function:** anger prevents me from feeling vulnerable pain
- **Cost:** grief remains unprocessed; compassion for myself stays blocked

## Breakthrough Moment
I had a clear and intense moment of feeling grief and compassion for myself in a way I had not experienced before.

## Psychological Interpretation
- Anger may function as a protector against helplessness or sadness.
- The ego prefers anger because it feels active, powerful, and justified.
- Grief feels softer, more exposed, and less controllable.
- When grief is allowed, self-compassion becomes accessible.

## Jungian Frame
- **Shadow content:** vulnerable grief, neediness, hurt, softness
- **Persona defense:** anger, strength, accusation, control
- **Integration task:** let anger become a doorway into grief instead of a wall against it

## Practice Rule
When anger appears, ask:

1. What grief is underneath this?
2. What part of me needs compassion right now?
3. What pain am I protecting myself from feeling?
4. Can I stay with the sadness for 30 seconds without converting it into blame?

## Mantra
Anger protects the wound. Grief reveals it. Compassion begins healing it.
