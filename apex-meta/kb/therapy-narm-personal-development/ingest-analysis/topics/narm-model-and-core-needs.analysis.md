## Macro

NARM is useful to this KB because it provides a theory-led, non-diagnostic language for examining how early relational conditions, core needs, bodily regulation, shame, contact, and adaptive strategies may shape present patterns. The primary source emphasizes resources, current functioning, and gradual regulation rather than reducing a person to symptoms or history.

## Meso

The primary source names five core needs—contact, attunement, trust, autonomy, and love/sexuality—and describes adaptive survival structures when early need fulfilment is disrupted. It links contact, identity, self-regulation, shame, counter-identification, and relational capacity. Contextual personal sources may later be compared to these concepts, but they cannot establish a NARM classification or diagnosis.

## Micro

For practice, the source emphasizes present-moment and somatic attention, resource orientation, noticing contact and disconnection, containment of affect, titration, orientation, grounding, pendulation, and using organized adult capacities rather than forcing regression or catharsis. The source itself cautions that trauma work can overwhelm or retraumatize when it is insufficiently titrated or resourced.

## Target answers

### narm-q01

In the primary NARM source, the five core needs are contact, attunement, trust, autonomy, and love/sexuality. The text presents them as biologically grounded needs and linked adult capacities; when early fulfilment is disrupted, it describes adaptive survival strategies that can organize around shame, defensive counter-identification, disconnection, and distorted present experience. This is theory from the source, not evidence that any particular person has a given survival style.

### narm-q02

The source describes a resource-oriented, present-focused, somatically informed approach: noticing contact and disconnection; somatic mindfulness; containment of affect; grounding and orientation; titration and pendulation; tracking nervous-system shifts; and using organized adult capacities to approach disorganized material gradually. It frames these as therapeutic methods and cautions that open attention or trauma work can overwhelm people when regulation is poor or work is not sufficiently titrated and resourced. Personal notes can identify questions or preferences for later work, but they do not independently validate a method's safety or suitability.

### narm-q03

The source contrasts shock trauma with developmental trauma: shock trauma is presented as a response to a discrete, bounded event, while developmental trauma concerns early experience that significantly affects subsequent development. It also describes attachment trauma as harm arising in close caregiving relationships. The distinction matters for the source's approach because it emphasizes early relational organization, core needs, identity, contact, and gradual regulation rather than only revisiting a single event. Any relevance to the operator remains a question for careful reflection or qualified professional exploration, not a conclusion from these materials.

## Contradictions

- The NARM source is a psychotherapeutic theory source, while surrender, ACIM, Jungian, and personal documents use different assumptions and cannot be treated as interchangeable evidence.
- Personal formulations describe possible patterns but repeatedly state that they are not formal diagnoses; they must not be promoted into NARM classifications without further evidence and professional context.

## Uncertainties

- The corpus does not contain an independent clinical assessment, so no personal survival-style classification is supported.
- The primary NARM source is largely German and book-derived; terminology needs careful preservation in later compiled pages.
- The source corpus contains several personal versions and working formulations; later topics must retain version and evidence-state distinctions.
