# Phase 1 topic analysis — NARM, Jungian, Surrender, Hawkins, ACIM, and Practical-Method Integration

## Macro

The frameworks can be complementary only as distinct layers: NARM for regulation/contact and paced therapeutic inquiry; Jungian-style shadow reflection for held-off affect and meaning; surrender/Hawkins and ACIM for a voluntary release or meaning practice; and intention encoding for concrete action design. None validates the others or replaces real-world discernment, boundaries, medical care, or therapy.

## Meso

Main risks are spiritual bypassing, forced forgiveness, treating surrender as consent or renewed access, using shadow work to invalidate anger, and using practical systems to outrun capacity. NARM's resource orientation and pacing, the personal sources' boundary focus, and explicit non-diagnostic/professional-care language form the guardrails.

## Micro

Reflective operating hypothesis: pause and regulate; name facts, emotion, needs, and boundary; allow grief or shadow inquiry only voluntarily and paced; decide discernment and access separately; use surrender only for the internal struggle one chooses to release; then translate the chosen next step into a small reversible action. Pause and seek professional support for overwhelming activation, safety risk, or inability to function.

## Target answers

### integration-q01

The frameworks overlap in attention to experience and intentional response, but remain distinct: NARM supplies present-focused regulation/contact and titration; shadow reflection can ask what is disowned or protected; surrender/Hawkins and ACIM can offer a voluntary release practice; practical intention encoding turns a chosen value or boundary into a small action. The compatible sequence is regulation first, interpretation second, action third—not one unified theory.

### integration-q02

Risks include using release language to deny injury, demand forgiveness, abandon discernment, suppress protective anger, or reopen access to unsafe people; treating a personal shadow insight as fact; and using high-intensity practices beyond capacity. Guardrails: distinguish inner release from reconciliation and renewed access; check facts and boundaries before spiritual interpretation; use short paced practices; preserve uncertainty; and seek professional support where distress, safety, or health concerns exceed self-guided work.

### integration-q03

Integrated sequence: 1) pause/orient/regulate; 2) record facts, body state, emotion, protection, and need; 3) assess current boundary and safety; 4) allow brief paced grief or shadow reflection if chosen; 5) choose whether any surrender/release practice is appropriate without changing the boundary; 6) choose a compassionate, concrete, reversible next action; 7) review impact and seek support when activation or risk is high. This is a reflective operating hypothesis, not treatment or a promise of outcome.
