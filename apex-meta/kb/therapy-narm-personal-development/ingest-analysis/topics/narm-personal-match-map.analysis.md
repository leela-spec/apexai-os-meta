## Macro

The evidence supports a NARM-informed reflection map, not a personal NARM classification. The highest correspondence is between NARM's broad concerns with contact, trust, regulation, and adaptive protection and repeated personal reports of relational threat, exclusion or betrayal sensitivity, anger/grief sequences, protective analysis or presentation, and a wish for connection with boundaries. NARM theory supplies the concepts; personal sources supply observations; neither alone proves a developmental history or survival style.

## Meso

High-confidence correspondence means multiple personal sources repeat an observation and that it clearly falls within a NARM concept category; it does not mean clinical certainty. Contact/trust and regulation are high as exploration domains. Autonomy and attunement/recognition are medium: they recur in insight notes but need clearer behavioral examples and exceptions. Shame and any named survival adaptation are low: some language is present, but the corpus lacks sufficient independent assessment, developmental detail, and counterevidence. Love/sexuality has no usable personal evidence in this corpus and should not be inferred.

## Micro

The most useful targets are present-focused and low-load: track a trigger-to-protection-to-need sequence; identify whether present boundary information, older threat expectation, physical depletion, or a mixture is active; practice regulation before consequential relational decisions; and test connection and trust through small, consent-based, reversible steps. These targets fit the evidence, stated goals, and self-reported capacity limits; they do not promise symptom resolution or replace therapy or health care.
