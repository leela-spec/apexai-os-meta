## Macro

The best-supported operating system is low-load, paced, present-focused and safety-bounded. It prioritizes regulation and discernment before interpretation, release, or action; it treats practices as experiments rather than cures.

## Meso

Highest-priority methods are: brief orienting/grounding and pause; structured trigger-protection-need logging; boundary and fact check before communication; small reversible values-based action; and therapist-supported exploration for high activation or grief. Surrender and shadow practices are optional adjuncts, not substitutes for safety or boundaries.

## Micro

Acute protocol: stop escalation; orient to surroundings and body; reduce input; use a brief grounding practice; record facts/emotion/urge/need; defer consequential messages; choose immediate safety/support; return later to boundary and action design. Daily: short check-in, one regulation practice, one small values action. Weekly: review triggers, capacity, connection and adjustments. Seek professional or emergency help for immediate safety risk, inability to regulate, or severe functional deterioration.

## Target answers

### operating-q01

Ranked methods: 1) brief regulation/orientation and delayed consequential action—high support, low energy cost, high safety; 2) trigger–protection–need map—high support, low cost, high learning value; 3) fact/boundary/discernment check—high personal fit and safety; 4) small reversible intention-based action—medium-high support and low-to-moderate cost; 5) paced grief or shadow inquiry—medium support but higher emotional cost, best with support; 6) surrender practice—contextual, optional, and never a substitute for the preceding steps.

### operating-q02

Safest source-bounded acute protocol: pause; orient and ground; reduce stimulation; name observable facts, body state, emotion, urge and immediate need; do not send consequential messages or make access decisions while highly activated; use a prechosen support or safety step; revisit boundaries and next action after regulation. If there is immediate danger, inability to stay safe, severe overwhelm, or major functional loss, use professional or emergency support rather than self-guided processing.

### operating-q03

Daily low-load practice: a brief capacity check, one grounding/orientation practice, and one sentence each for trigger, protection, need and next small action. Weekly: review patterns, boundaries, connection, and energy; select one reversible experiment. Grief/self-compassion inquiry should be brief, voluntary, and stopped if activation rises. The practices are reflective supports, not treatment.

### operating-q04

Staged path: stabilize and observe; build boundary/relationship discernment; practice small connection and values actions; explore grief, trust and deeper hypotheses with paced support; then revise the system from evidence of what is sustainable. The path is adaptive and capacity-sensitive, not a cure plan.
