## 14. Practical Protocols to Use

### 14.1 The 3-Minute Courtroom Exit

```yaml
trigger: "Need to prove the case / correct the narrative."
steps:
  - stand or sit upright
  - exhale slowly
  - say: "This is the need to be right."
  - say: "I already know what happened."
  - say: "Truth does not need her agreement."
  - write one boundary lesson
  - choose one constructive action
rule: "No internal court case longer than 3 minutes."
```

### 14.2 System Reclassification Drill

```yaml
trigger: "Urge to fix, explain, teach, or force accountability."
steps:
  - classify the system:
      type_1: "correctable: can reflect, repair, learn"
      type_2: "limited: partial awareness, inconsistent"
      type_3: "non-correctable in current context: deflects, distorts, collapses, avoids accountability"
  - if type_3:
      - say: "Correction will not work here."
      - say: "Engaging equals energy loss."
      - redirect precision to startup, teaching, training, or compatible people
```

### 14.3 Hawkins-Style Anger Surrender

```yaml
trigger: "Strong belly anger."
steps:
  - bring situation to mind only to 60-70 percent intensity
  - locate sensation in belly
  - drop the story: "No story, only sensation."
  - say: "I allow this feeling completely."
  - if resistance appears: "I allow the resistance too."
  - stay until sensation shifts, softens, spreads, or decreases
caution: "Do not use this to rehearse the story. The object is sensation, not the accusation."
```

### 14.4 Warrior Release Protocol

```yaml
trigger: "High physical anger, urge to go to war."
steps:
  - stand in martial posture
  - feet grounded, chest open, jaw relaxed
  - bring anger to 60-70 percent
  - exhale forcefully and slowly 10-15 times
  - low voice:
      - "This is anger."
      - "This is power."
      - "This is mine."
      - "I do not need the story."
  - redirect into pushups, walk, shadowboxing, or cold water
  - close with: "Done."
```
