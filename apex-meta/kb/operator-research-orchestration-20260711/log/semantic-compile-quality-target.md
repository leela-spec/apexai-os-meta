# Semantic Compile Quality Target

## Purpose

Produce a dense, source-grounded decision system that another AI can retrieve, trust, and act on without rereading the entire raw corpus.

## Non-Negotiable Content Standard

- Depth: Every Phase 1 analysis contains substantive source-specific reasoning; headings, placeholders, and one-sentence summaries are not acceptable.
- Grounding: Every material conclusion carries an exact raw-source pointer.
- Extraction: Capture decisions, mechanisms, workflows, roles, artifacts, inputs, outputs, constraints, failure modes, assumptions, and unresolved questions when present.
- Translation: Classify historical material as transferable pattern, historical/model-specific assumption, Claude-native decision, rejected pattern, or insufficient evidence.
- Connection: State corroborating, conflicting, superseding, dependency, and reuse relationships to other sources and proposed compiled pages.
- Efficiency: Prefer compact structured claims and high-information synthesis over copied prose; preserve raw references for detail recovery.

## Corpus Overlap / Convergence Target

- Normalize: Treat equivalent processes, designs, roles, workflows, decisions, artifacts, and constraints as one candidate pattern while preserving each source's wording and scope.
- Evidence: Record independent supporting sources, refinements, contradictions, and supersession evidence for every material candidate.
- Ranking: Classify each candidate as high-evidence core pattern, strong use-case-specific pattern, historical proposal, contested pattern, or single-source hypothesis.
- Scoring Inputs: Use independent recurrence, source authority, recency, operational specificity, reuse value, and contradiction penalties.
- Output: Each compiled core-pattern or decision page must name its evidence set, why the sources are independent or non-independent, the use cases it fits, and the Claude-native recommendation.

## Iteration Gate

- Before drafting: Read the raw source, its manifest record, Phase 0 maps, and already-completed related analyses.
- During drafting: Add only source-grounded material; make cross-source links explicit as they are discovered.
- Before advancing: Check that the analysis answers what the source establishes, why it matters, what connects, what Claude should adopt or avoid, and when raw evidence must be reopened.
- Before query-ready: Reject pages that are thin, generic, unlinked, pointerless, or unable to distinguish recurring evidence from a single-source idea.
