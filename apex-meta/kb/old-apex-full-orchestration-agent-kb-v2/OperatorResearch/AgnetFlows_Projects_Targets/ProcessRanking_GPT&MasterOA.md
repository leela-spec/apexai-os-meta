# Ranking: processes most useful for automating the Master of Arts workflow examples

**Main conclusion:** the best “top 10” is not the ten highest raw process scores. It is the ten-process **coverage set** that can automate the widest range of your Master of Arts workflows: idea capture, project prioritization, workshop creation, Leela use-case translation, content/media pipelines, self-employment/legal operations, handoffs, recurring reviews, and Hermes self-buildout.

The attached workflow database itself already shows this variety: it shortlists **Hermes self-buildout**, **source-to-workflow normalization**, **Master of Arts project matrix**, **raw idea database entry**, **workshop outline pipeline**, **architecture slide compression**, **embodied drill protocol**, **Leela use-case translation**, **self-employment setup**, and **specialized agent handover** as the core workflow examples. It also marks the strongest source domains: agent swarm testing, content creation, workshop analysis, Peaceful Warrior/Superhero Training, Audio-to-Idea Database, Master of Arts Meta, legal/self-employment setup, dating handover, cacao scripts, and martial arts technique analysis.

## Selection logic

I ranked by:

|Criterion|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Coverage**|How many of your workflow examples the process can support.|
|**Complementarity**|Whether it adds a different capability instead of duplicating another process.|
|**Hermes fit**|Whether it maps cleanly to Skill, Kanban, Cron, `delegate_task`, or context files.|
|**Evidence / maturity**|Whether it is a known process model or already encoded in our process pack.|
|**Risk control**|Whether it prevents drift, unsafe automation, weak handoffs, or chaos.|

The external grounding supports keeping a mixed portfolio: Double Diamond is a general design-process frame for design and innovation work, Scrum emphasizes iterative/incremental inspection and adaptation, NIST AI RMF uses Govern/Map/Measure/Manage for AI risk, ReAct interleaves reasoning and action for tool-using agents, and CRISP-DM structures data/evidence projects across phases. ([designcouncil.org.uk](https://www.designcouncil.org.uk/resources/the-double-diamond/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "The Double Diamond - Design Council"))

# Why these ten beat the raw top-scored list

The raw process index gives very high scores to several processes: `PRC-CORE-001`, `PRC-VERIFY-001`, `PRC-MULTI-001`, `PRC-HANDOFF-001`, `PRC-RISK-001`, and `PRC-SYS-001` all score strongly on impact/evidence/risk. But if we only rank by those scores, the set becomes too verification- and artifact-heavy. For your Master of Arts workflow database, we also need **portfolio routing**, **data extraction**, **creative divergence**, **system design**, **iteration**, and **runtime orchestration**.

That is why I rank `PRC-DATA-001`, `PRC-DIV-001`, `PRC-SYS-001`, and `PRC-SCRUM-001` higher than some individually strong but narrower processes. The attached workflow examples explicitly include raw-idea ingestion, idea clustering/project mapping, workshop creation, safety review, architecture compression, theory-to-content pipeline, project-to-execution matrix, offer development, media planning, Leela use-case translation, coaching format definition, and legal setup.

# Best supplemental relationships

## A. Master creation stack

This is the stack for creating serious artifacts: workshop specs, Leela use cases, process records, concept documents, and guides.

```yaml
stack_name: master_creation_stack
use_for:
  - workshop outlines
  - Leela use-case specs
  - content assets
  - guide creation
  - workflow records
processes:
  - PRC-CORE-001
  - PRC-DIV-001
  - PRC-DATA-001
  - PRC-VERIFY-001
relationship:
  - PRC-DATA gathers and structures inputs.
  - PRC-DIV frames the problem and options.
  - PRC-CORE creates the artifact.
  - PRC-VERIFY checks factuality, source fidelity, and goal fit.
```

**Example:** raw note → idea database entry → concept options → workshop outline → verified working artifact.
