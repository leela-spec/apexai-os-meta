## Definition
This concept combines three source layers. The NARM source supports anger/protest as a survival-relevant response to unmet needs and describes integrated anger opening access to longing, pain, and grief. The personal shadow note states that anger, frustration, and irritation often cover grief, and that self-compassion becomes accessible when grief is allowed. The handover source treats this as a working intervention priority, not a diagnosis. Source pointers: `ET-Heller-NARM.md:289-307`, `ET-Heller-NARM.md:5009-5021`, `shadow_insight_v1.md:Core Insight`, `PsychologicalHandover_ChatTherapeuticFramework_inACIM.md:3`.
