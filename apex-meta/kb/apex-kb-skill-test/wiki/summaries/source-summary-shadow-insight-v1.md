## Core Summary
This self-report source states that anger, frustration, and irritation often cover grief. It frames anger as a protective state that blocks vulnerable pain, and says self-compassion becomes accessible when grief is allowed. Its practice rule asks what grief, compassion need, or protected pain is underneath anger, and whether sadness can be felt briefly without converting it into blame.
