# Candidate Is Not Accepted Truth

## Definition

Apex handoff contracts distinguish four distinct claim states along a ladder: raw source evidence, candidate output, validated output, and accepted truth. No stage may be silently skipped. A candidate output — whether generated by a model, an agent, or a workflow — remains non-authoritative until it has been validated and explicitly accepted; it may not be treated as canonical simply because it exists or reads plausibly. This ladder is named directly by the `handoff_contract_index` core question `source_evidence_vs_candidate_vs_validated_vs_accepted_truth` in the Phase 2 specialized-index compile plan, and is reinforced by Phase 1 batch 04's claims about promotion caution.

## Operating Rules

```yaml
rules:
  - "Every output that could inform doctrine, status, or a next action carries an explicit claim/status label (raw source, candidate, validated, or accepted)."
  - "A candidate-labeled output blocks downstream code or agents from treating it as canonical until it passes validation."
  - "The validation step must be visible and attributable — it is not implied by the mere existence of the candidate."
  - "Only accepted-status items may be relied upon by downstream skills without independent re-verification."
  - "Raw source quotes preserved for evidentiary purposes are not, by that fact alone, promoted to accepted claims."
```

## Macro / Meso / Micro

### Macro

The compile plan makes claim provenance a first-class `handoff_contract_index` concern because Apex mixes generated candidate content with human-approved doctrine throughout its lifecycle. Without a labeled ladder from evidence to accepted truth, agents downstream cannot tell which parts of a KB, handoff packet, or execution recap are safe to build on without re-checking.

### Meso

This is the general mechanism behind the operator-gate pattern (B04-C005) and the promotion-caution claims (B04-C015 through B04-C017), applied specifically to claim status rather than to file writes. It is the same discipline this very KB's own Phase 1 frontmatter enforces with its `claim_labels_allowed` and `operator_gate` fields, and the same discipline the operator's own review-decision log applies when it explicitly separates `write_as_doctrine` from `write_as_boundary_or_open_question` and `avoid_in_phase2`.

### Micro

B04-C015 states that external/runtime/platform claims default to future-research status and must not become accepted doctrine without independent verification. B04-C016 (labeled interpretation, high confidence) frames Apex orchestration as a source-locked artifact lifecycle: deterministic navigation, Phase 1 semantic ingest, explicit operator gate, then Phase 2 synthesis only after approval — the ladder this page describes is the general form of that same gate. The compile plan's own `source_evidence_vs_candidate_vs_validated_vs_accepted_truth` question (line 77) is the direct naming source for this page's four-stage vocabulary.
